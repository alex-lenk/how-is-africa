Who We Are
While living in cities across East Africa, meeting fascinating people, making friends, breaking bread and finding new colleagues, many entrusted me with their unique stories. They could have been inspiring TedX Talks about life, courage, joy and unexpected perspectives. Those cities were boiling with vibrant energy, exciting lifestyles and urban cultures. Yet, in the media world, those narratives are overshadowed by accounts of social unrest and political turmoils. For years, I was bombarded with one question: “How is Africa?” The gap between what I saw and what many imagined Africa to be was so striking that I realised it was time to act. I brought passionate individuals together to tell stories from African cities, offer tools to young people to tell theirs and bring harmony to all these different voices together in one single platform. Karibu, as they say in Swahili! Welcome.
— Liza Bell

Our Team

  • Liza Bel / Founder
    Liza started off as a radio talk-show host on French Radio London, jumped to the digital world at AOL and went on to manage a project in East Africa supported by the Howard G. Buffett Foundation before joining Google UK, where she’s rolling out a social development initiative for young people. Liza was featured on multiple TV stations and oversees digital development of start-ups and foundations across Africa. Liza blogs on Huffington Post UK.
  • Jodie Williams / Film Editor
    Jodie was talent spotted by the production department of an international NGO. She’s currently working as editor and camera operator for a big news agency in London. Her passion for images is limitless.
  • Jean-Marie Manishimwe / Cinematographer
    JMM is a Rwandan filmmaker who has covered high profile events with the British Council, Peace One Day, the World Economic Forum and the Rwandan Government. He has also directed short documentaries such as “A world without Sound”.
  • Marc Gwamaka / Education specialist & Youth Leader
    Marc is responsible for the running of national youth leadership programmes in Rwanda and he has trained 1042 young people in the last 3 years. Marc has been active in the fight against genocide since his teenage years. He is the founder of Walk to Remember: an international programme that educates and mobilises youth from all over the world to fight against genocide. He is also an actor.
  • Freda Asiimwe / Production Manager
    Freda has an outstanding track record of managing protocol and ground operations for the UN, Médecins sans Frontières and Kampala International Airport. She oversees logistics for high-profile visits and complex staff moments and missions across East Africa.
  • Valentin Dusabe / Partnerships Coordinator
    Valentin is a law student in Brussels. Very early in his life Valentin successfully combined his studies with work and other commitments. Started in the archives in an hospital, to make a ‘career’ switch to the sales world in the store Hackett London and to end up currently as a football coach in Zulte Waregem. He recently joined How is Africa team to support us in growing our international network of partners, supporters and contributors.
